A Guide to Digital Accessibility

 

About this project

This project is the culmination of a class-wide collaboration for an Advanced Technical Writing course in the Professional Writing and Technical Communication (PWTC) certificate program at UMass Amherst. It aims to inform information designers on digital accessibility— what it is, who it benefits, and why it matters. A major focus of this project is to provide guidance and best practices for making accessible digital video content.

 

About me

My name is Stacy Shang, and I am an aspiring technical writer interested in user experience and digital accessibility. I am a rising senior Linguistics student at UMass Amherst and am working towards completing the PWTC certification program. As of writing this (May 2026), I am interning at the University of Michigan as a technical writer/instructional designer for the digital accessibility team at LSA Technology Services.

Frequently asked questions

How was the work divided for such a large project?

Though this project involved a lot of collaboration, each student has edited and outputted their own version of the content. The part that will vary the most from project to project is the “What is accessibility?” section, which we all authored independently. Each of the following sections on accessible digital video content has been authored by a different team of students within the class. I worked on “Video transitions” with my team members, Jenna Marino and Mira Kapoor. Each topic page has gone through several inter-team workshops and drafts to reach the state that you see them in now.

What skills and knowledge were gained through this project?

Through this project, I have definitely gained a deeper understanding of disability and accessibility concepts through the process of researching and writing for an audience that is new to accessibility. The teamwork/collaboration aspect of the project also helped me develop my communication, workshopping, and time management skills. Additionally, the process of creating this content itself was a practice in digital accessibility (adding alt text to images, using proper heading levels, checking web content with WAVE Web Accessibility Evaluation Tools).

What is MadCap Flare?

MadCap Flare is a software that was used to create the PDF and website output of this project. It is primarily used by technical writers to author content. The content can then be outputted in three formats: website, PDF, and EPUB. The draw of Flare to technical writers is its capacity for writers to reuse and update certain topic pages across many different documents. Flare also allows you to make changes to a topic page for select documents, but not for others— meaning you can customize pages depending who your audience is.
