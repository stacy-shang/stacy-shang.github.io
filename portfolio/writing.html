Writing

Essay on semantics in monkey calls

I wrote an essay exploring field studies conducted on the communication systems of various monkey species and the significance of their findings in regards to the existence of semantics in primates, as discussed in the book What It all Means by Philippe Schlenker.

Expository piece on approaches to studying African American Language

I wrote a paper on four main approaches that sociolinguists take to researching AAL mentioned in Walt Wolfram’s paper “The Sociolinguistic Construction of African American Language.” Specifically, I discuss how these approaches may perpetuate "sociolinguist myths" about the variety and how researchers can combat this issue in future studies.

View the essay (PDF)

The Woke Project

My African American English class collaborated on a poster presenting a unified overview of the term woke. My group focused on the current use of woke, starting from the 2016 Black Lives Matter movement, and how its connotations have deviated significantly from its original usage within the Black community.
